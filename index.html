Engines

Selected engines from the practice — samples of how the method thinks, not a catalog. Each is orchestrated, checked, timed, and human-gated; each runs live at its own address.

(anti)library

Sanskrit · मन्थन manthan · to churn.
The cosmic ocean, churned until treasures rise.

Open the engine ↗

A library is a self-portrait. The engine reads it.

Photograph your shelves. The engine catalogues every book, breaks each into its atomic ideas, and finds the threads running across the whole collection — the portrait you could not see from inside the frame.

MethodCollect · Classify · Churn · Curate · Consume
StatusLive — anti-library.ai

Napkin-Solo

English · compound.
A napkin sketch, drawn alone — and a nod to the reluctant hero who trusted his own judgment.

Open the engine ↗

The hardest part of visual thinking is not the drawing. It is knowing what to draw.

Feed it any text — a dense paper, a strategy brief, a tangled idea — and it extracts the one insight worth illustrating, then draws the napkin sketch that makes it land. No prompt engineering. The engine reads, thinks, and draws on its own.

StatusLive — napkinsolo.ai

Napkin Dōjō

Japanese · 道場 dōjō.
The place where you return, repeat, and the skill becomes yours.

Open the engine ↗

We read a lot and remember little.

You finish a book, highlight an article, save a thread — a week later it is gone. The Dōjō brings the best ideas back, again and again, until knowing about them becomes knowing them.

StatusLive on the web — napkinsolo.ai/dojo · App Store edition coming

Lagom

Swedish · lagom · from lag om, "around the team." Just the right amount, no more.

A business thinker — not a general-purpose one.

Lagom reasons about a business problem with the mental models that hold there — unit economics, second-order effects, incentives and moats, the shape of a market — settles the argument, then gives it form. It will not draw a single figure until the argument is complete.

About

For thirty years, one problem in many costumes: how people and organizations actually change.

Note Draft bio — career facts to be confirmed with Bharat before launch. Also Occasional investor — B Capital, Karmen AI, BeeCure, 4T2, 52TEN. Contact bharat@bharatkhandelwal.com

For thirty years I have worked on one problem in many costumes: how people and organizations actually change — not how they say they will, but what genuinely moves them from ambition to action.

That work now has a second half. I build AI engines that reason the way people do, and I help organizations get ready for the change AI asks of them. The two halves feed each other: the engines are disciplined by what I know about people, and the advisory work is grounded by what I learn building.

I live between the practice, the engines, the books — and a library of about seven hundred books that turned out to be a self-portrait.
